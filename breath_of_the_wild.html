About "The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild":

Available on the Nintendo Switch and Wii U platforms, "The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild" is a single-player, third-person video game, where the user becomes a character named Link. The game takes place in a large kingdom called The Kingdom of Hyrule. Although given the task of defeating the enemy, Ganon, who attempts to destroy Hyrule, the user has the freedom to do many other things. The game includes shrines, where puzzles, that lie hidden underneath the shrine, test the user's problem-solving skills; villages, where villagers provide tasks that come with rewards; forests, where the user can fight golems, lizards, and other creatures; and many other intriguing aspects.

A virtual time that moves 1 hour for every 1 minute in real life is presented in the game. Like the real world, the game gets dark during the night and turns bright during the day. There are also times during the game where the weather gets cloudy or stormy.

The user controls their character's action using a controller. Actions in the game resemble our everyday lives: the user can eat, walk, run, jump, sleep, and crouch. Some actions are not so common in real life, like hang gliding, swinging weapons, and climbing mountains.


Nintendo released "The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild" on March 3, 2017. The game was directed by Hidemaro Fujibayshi; Satoru Takizawa, the art director; and Takuhiro Dohta, the technical director. More information on Breath of the Wild can be found on the Nintendo Website.
